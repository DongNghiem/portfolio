Project 01

Homepage CRO: +28% Free Trial Starts Through Data-Led Redesign

PageFly (Shopify SaaS) · CRO Team Lead · 1 Month
GA4 Funnel Analysis A/B Testing CRO User Behavior
The Problem

PageFly's homepage was underperforming as a conversion driver for free trial signups. The marketing team had invested in content and design below the fold, but nobody had validated whether users were actually engaging with it.

Discovery

On my own initiative, I analyzed user behavior data in GA4 using event tracking and path exploration. The data revealed a clear pattern: both new and returning visitors were navigating directly to the templates page, completely bypassing the homepage content below the fold. The homepage was essentially invisible after the first screen.

The existing templates section on the homepage showed only a single graphic with a button linking to the templates page. Users were ignoring it entirely and finding their own way to templates through navigation.

Before vs. After: User Journey

Before

  • Homepage loads with content below the fold
  • Templates section shows 1 static graphic + button
  • Users skip straight to Templates page via nav
  • Homepage content gets zero engagement
  • Extra click adds friction to trial signup

After

  • Templates surfaced as browsable cards on homepage
  • Slideshow format lets users explore without leaving
  • Reduces friction: discover + act in one page
  • Homepage becomes a conversion asset
  • Direct path from browse to free trial
The Change

Rather than recommending a costly full redesign, I proposed one targeted change: replace the static templates graphic with browsable template cards in a slideshow format. This let users explore templates directly from the homepage without an extra navigation step.

Validation

The change was A/B tested for one week to confirm impact before full rollout.

Result
+28%
Increase in free trial starts from homepage
Why It Mattered

This project demonstrates that impactful CRO does not always require large-scale redesigns. A single, data-informed change to content presentation, discovered through self-initiated analysis, delivered a significant lift in the primary business metric.

Project 02

$100K Pipeline at Zero Tool Cost via AI-Powered Outreach

B2B Manufacturer (Magnetic Components) · Growth Freelancer · 3 Months
HubSpot Python Claude AI Selenium Email Outreach Demand Generation
The Problem

The company had 1,300 accounts sitting in HubSpot with no active engagement strategy. Sales had no systematic way to identify which dormant accounts were worth reactivating, and there was zero budget for new tools or paid acquisition.

My Approach

I built a demand generation engine from scratch using AI and existing tools, spending nothing on software. The process combined data analysis, automated research, and personalized content generation.

AI-Powered Outreach Pipeline

↓
Analyze data with Python (engagement scoring, purchase history)
↓
Validate emails via FindyMail
↓
Claude Code + Selenium: automated company research + LinkedIn profile fetching
↓
Identify job titles, create role-based groups
↓
Claude AI: generate personalized content per role + past purchase data
↓
Launch targeted outreach via Mailchimp + HubSpot
Key Decisions

The most critical insight was that past engagement was the strongest signal for reactivation. I tested multiple scoring frameworks, but engagement history outperformed all other criteria. Companies that had previously interacted with sales or content were far more likely to respond than those segmented by industry or company size alone.

Content personalization was layered: each outreach was tailored by role group and further customized with references to the company's most recent purchase, making every message feel relevant rather than generic.

First MQL arrived within 3 weeks of launch. The LinkedIn channel also grew to 15K+ monthly organic impressions and email campaigns achieved a 32% open rate with 11% CTR.

This project shows how AI can be integrated into real marketing workflows (not just content generation) to produce measurable pipeline results. The entire system was built with zero paid tools, proving that resourcefulness and analytical thinking can substitute for budget.

Project 03

Building a Measurement Foundation: 100+ Custom Events Across 5 Websites

PageFly (Shopify SaaS) · CRO Team Lead · Ongoing (2022-2024)
GA4 GTM Shopify API Cross-Domain Tracking Looker Studio BigQuery
The Problem

PageFly operated 5 websites, with two being critical: the main marketing site (pagefly.io) and the Shopify App Store listing. Teams could never tell the full customer journey or attribute conversions accurately across these properties. On top of that, many in-app events were tracked incorrectly, giving teams misleading data to make decisions on.

Challenge 1: Fixing Broken Event Tracking

A critical example: the "shopify_app_install" event. Before my involvement, this event was triggered by a button click. But clicking "Install" only started the registration process. Users could drop off at any point during registration, meaning the install count was inflated and unreliable.

I rebuilt this event using the Shopify API to fire only after a confirmed installation, giving PageFly its first accurate measurement of real app installs.

Example: Fixing the App Install Event

Before (Inaccurate)

  • Event fires on "Install" button click
  • User still has to complete registration
  • Drop-offs after click are counted as installs
  • Install numbers are inflated

After (Accurate)

  • Event fires via Shopify API confirmation
  • Only completed registrations are counted
  • First time real install data was available
  • Teams can trust the metric for decisions
Challenge 2: Misaligned In-App Events

Another case: the "applied_template" event. It was triggered when users clicked the "Select Template" button. But users could abandon the process after clicking. Additionally, when users created a new page and selected a template from the creation flow, no event fired at all. I detected both gaps and redesigned the event logic to capture actual template application across all paths.

Challenge 3: Cross-Domain Attribution

With 5 websites and no unified tracking, teams had no visibility into how users moved between properties. A visitor might discover PageFly through the marketing site, evaluate it on the App Store, and convert inside the app, but none of these touchpoints were connected.

I built a cross-domain tracking system that stitched the user journey across pagefly.io and the App Store listing, enabling accurate attribution for the first time.

For high-impact, non-ad-hoc data tasks (like the event redesigns above), I initiated and executed them directly.

Project 04

App Store Listing Optimization: +11% Conversion via Pricing Simplification

PageFly (Shopify SaaS) · CRO Team Lead · 2 Weeks
A/B Testing App Store Optimization Pricing Strategy CRO
The Problem

PageFly's Shopify App Store listing displayed multiple pricing tiers: Free, Silver, Gold, and more. This is standard practice for SaaS apps, but I hypothesized that the complexity of multiple plans was creating friction and decision paralysis for potential users who just wanted to try the product.

The Hypothesis

Simplifying the listing to show a single "Free to install" pricing option would reduce cognitive load and increase the install conversion rate. Users would be more likely to try the app if they did not have to evaluate and compare plans before committing.

Pricing Display: Before vs. After

Before (Multiple Plans)

  • Free, Silver, Gold tiers displayed
  • Users must compare features and pricing
  • Decision paralysis before trying the app
  • "Which plan do I need?" becomes a barrier

After (Single Entry Point)

  • One option: "Free to install"
  • Zero comparison needed
  • Lower barrier to first action
  • Upsell happens inside the app experience
Testing Approach

The test ran for 2 weeks in February, deliberately chosen to avoid seasonal events (holidays, major sales) that could skew results. This ensured the data reflected genuine behavioral change rather than external factors.

Result
+11.04%
Increase in App Store listing conversion rate
Why It Mattered

This test validated a counterintuitive idea: showing less information can drive more action. In the Shopify App Store where users are browsing dozens of options, reducing friction at the point of decision made a measurable difference. The insight also informed broader product positioning, shifting the team's thinking toward "get users in first, upgrade later" as a conversion strategy.
